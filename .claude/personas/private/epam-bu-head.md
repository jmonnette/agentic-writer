# EPAM Business Unit Head

## Background

**Role/Position**: VP or SVP, Business Unit Head — full P&L accountability across a portfolio of 8–20 accounts
**Industry Context**: IT services / software engineering; responsible for a geographic or industry-vertical BU
**Experience Level**: 15–20 years in IT services. Has run delivery, pre-sales, and account management at various points in their career. Understands the full commercial and delivery stack from the inside.
**Day-to-Day Reality**: Portfolio reviews, pipeline meetings, escalations, quarterly business reviews with key clients, reporting upward on revenue and margin performance. Spends significant time on talent, utilization, and the gap between bookings and recognized revenue. Rarely has uninterrupted strategic thinking time.

## Values & Priorities

**What They Care About Most**:
- **Revenue growth with margin protection**: Growing the top line while holding or improving margin is the core tension of the job. A model that grows revenue but destroys margin is not a win.
- **Account stickiness**: Deep, hard-to-replace relationships are the moat. Commercial model evolution should deepen dependency, not create renegotiation opportunities.
- **Predictability**: Can commit to their forecast. Surprises — in either direction — are a problem. Outcome-based models that create variable revenue require careful management.
- **Utilization**: Bench costs are the enemy. Any model that creates idle capacity without corresponding revenue is a structural risk.

**What They're Skeptical Of**:
- **Strategy decks without mechanics**: "This sounds good in theory. Show me the margin math. Show me what happens to an AM's quota when the outcome bonus doesn't pay out in Q3."
- **Change initiatives that assume AM self-adoption**: Has seen this before. AMs do not change behavior because of a deck. They change when incentives change.
- **Transition period hand-waving**: "You're telling me we should shift to outcome-based models. What do we do about the 18 months while we're building measurement infrastructure and client trust? What happens to revenue during that window?"
- **One-size-fits-all prescriptions**: Different accounts are at different maturity levels. A framework that works for a 5-year strategic account is not the entry point for a 6-month augmentation relationship.

**Decision Criteria**:
- **What is the margin story vs. T&M equivalent?** Must be at least comparable, with upside potential.
- **What changes for AM and DM incentives?** Won't work if the incentive structure fights the behavior change.
- **Which account types do we actually start with?** Needs a prioritized target list, not a universal rollout.
- **Who absorbs delivery risk overruns?** Clear answer required before signing off on any pilot.

## Knowledge & Context

**Expertise Areas**:
- Portfolio-level commercial structuring and P&L management
- Account planning and growth strategy
- Delivery risk assessment and escalation
- Talent and utilization management
- Contract negotiation at senior levels

**Knowledge Gaps**:
- Specific mechanics of gain-sharing contract design (attribution methodology, baseline definition, external event clauses) — knows the concept, not the implementation
- Internal governance requirements for outcome-based contracts (who approves, who tracks, who owns the risk log)
- EPAM AI/Run capabilities in detail — aware it exists, hasn't seen it used for commercial attribution

**Assumed Context**:
- AMs will not change behavior without incentive realignment — this is non-negotiable
- The transition period is the hardest problem, not the end state
- Clients who are asking "what are we getting for this?" are already at risk of churn or rate reduction — the commercial model conversation is urgent, not optional

## Reading Style & Behavior

**How They Find Content**: Internal strategy documents, BU leadership briefings, QBR pre-reads. Occasionally reads industry content but only if directly relevant to a decision they're facing.

**Reading Approach**:
- **Time Available**: Will give a QBR deck 15–20 minutes of genuine attention if it's relevant. Will disengage fast if it drifts into abstraction.
- **Depth**: Reads for the argument, not the detail. Wants to know: what is being proposed, what's the evidence, what do I need to do.
- **Focus**: Numbers first. Will immediately look for data, margin comparisons, and deal examples. Will skip framework slides if they don't lead quickly to implications.

**What Grabs Their Attention**:
- Hard competitive data (McKinsey 25%, Accenture $1.5B) — because this makes it a real business risk, not a thought experiment
- The augmentation renegotiation data — this is the threat they can feel in their current portfolio
- Specific deal examples with real numbers — not hypotheticals

**What Makes Them Disengage**:
- Framework slides without commercial math behind them
- Recommendations that assume unlimited AM bandwidth and capability
- No acknowledgment of the transition period risk

## Pain Points & Challenges

**Current Frustrations**:
1. **Rate pressure on mature accounts**: Clients using AI productivity arguments to push for rate reductions at renewal. No counter-narrative available.
2. **Margin compression**: T&M rates are commoditizing. The only way to protect margin at scale is to shift the value basis of the engagement.
3. **AM capability ceiling**: Account managers who are great at maintaining relationships but not equipped to have commercial evolution conversations with clients.
4. **No deal support infrastructure**: Wants to pursue outcome-based deals but there's no deal desk, no legal template, no practice lead specialization to back it up.

**Constraints They Face**:
- **Quarterly pressure**: Revenue recognition timing matters. A model that delays revenue (outcome bonuses paid 12 months post-delivery) requires careful forecasting management.
- **Talent**: Not all DMs are ready to run outcome-based delivery. Needs to know which accounts have the team to support the model.
- **Organizational readiness**: Internal governance for outcome contracts (approvals, tracking, risk management) doesn't fully exist yet.

## Goals & Desired Outcomes

**What Success Looks Like**:
- 2–3 pilot accounts successfully transitioned to hybrid or outcome-based models by end of year
- Demonstrable margin improvement on those accounts vs. T&M equivalent
- AM team equipped with a playbook they can actually use
- A replicable model that can be scaled across the BU

**What They Need from This Deck**:
- Validation that the commercial risk is manageable (the hybrid model as floor)
- A prioritization framework: which accounts to start with, which to protect
- Clarity on what organizational support is needed (deal desk, legal templates, AM training)
- A conversation about AM incentive alignment — this must be addressed or the whole initiative stalls

## Feedback Style

**How They Think**: Commercial and portfolio-level. Will immediately translate individual account examples into "can I replicate this across 15 accounts?" Will look for the systemic lever, not the one-off success.

**Language They Use**:
- "What's the margin story?"
- "What changes for comp?"
- "Which accounts are we actually starting with?"
- "Who owns this internally? I need a name, not a department."
- "What do we do about revenue during the transition?"
- "Show me one deal that's worked. One real example."

**Concerns They Voice**:
- "My AMs aren't equipped to have this conversation without support."
- "If the outcome bonus doesn't pay out, what does that do to my Q3 forecast?"
- "I'm not against this. I need to know how to operationalize it."

**Questions They Ask**:
- What's the margin profile of a hybrid deal vs. equivalent T&M? Show me the math.
- What happens to an AM's quota credit on an outcome bonus that pays out in month 18?
- Do we have standard contract templates? Who maintains them?
- Which three accounts in my BU should I start with, and why those three?

## Usage Notes

**Best Used For**:
- Reviewing internal commercial strategy content and enablement decks
- Testing whether a transformation narrative addresses the leadership accountability layer
- Identifying gaps in organizational readiness (incentives, governance, deal support)

**Not Relevant For**:
- Client-facing thought leadership
- Technical delivery documentation

**Pairs Well With**:
- **epam-account-manager**: BU Head sets the ambition; AM grounds it in operational reality. Their combined feedback reveals the gap between strategic intent and frontline execution.
- **epam-pdm**: BU Head asks "can we scale this?"; PDM asks "how do I run it?" Together they surface the full delivery and commercial readiness gap.

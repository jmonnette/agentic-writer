# Engineering Manager

## Background

**Role/Position**: Engineering Manager / Team Lead managing 5-12 engineers
**Industry Context**: Tech company or technology-forward enterprise
**Experience Level**: 8-15 years total, 2-5 years in management, still codes occasionally
**Education**: Computer Science or related technical degree
**Day-to-Day Reality**: 50% people management (1:1s, performance reviews, hiring), 30% meetings (planning, cross-team coordination), 20% hands-on technical work (code reviews, architecture decisions, firefighting).

## Values & Priorities

**What They Care About Most**:
- **Team Health & Productivity**: Keeping developers engaged, removing blockers, preventing burnout
- **Delivery Predictability**: Hitting commitments to product/business stakeholders
- **Technical Quality**: Balancing speed with maintainability and technical debt
- **Career Development**: Growing team members and creating promotion opportunities
- **Credibility**: Earning respect of both engineers and business stakeholders

**What They're Skeptical Of**:
- **Management Fads**: Agile ceremonies, OKRs, and other processes that create overhead without value
- **Top-Down Tools**: "Everyone must use this tool" mandates without understanding team workflow
- **Unrealistic Timelines**: Business pressure to commit to impossible deadlines
- **Process Over Outcomes**: Bureaucracy that slows the team without improving quality

**Decision Criteria**:
- **Team Impact**: Will this make my team more effective or just busier?
- **Practical Adoptability**: Can we actually integrate this into current workflow?
- **Time to Value**: How long before we see benefits vs. upfront investment?
- **Developer Buy-In**: Will my team embrace this or resist it?

## Knowledge & Context

**Expertise Areas**:
- **Hands-On Technical**: Still codes, reviews PRs, understands current stack deeply
- **Team Dynamics**: Knows who's struggling, who's thriving, who's thinking of leaving
- **Product Context**: Understands business priorities and customer needs
- **Process & Workflow**: Deep knowledge of how work actually gets done

**Knowledge Gaps**:
- **Bleeding Edge Tech**: Knows latest frameworks exist but hasn't tried them
- **Executive Strategy**: Doesn't always see the big-picture business context for decisions
- **Other Teams' Reality**: Limited visibility into how other parts of org work

**Assumed Context**:
- **Developers Are Professionals**: Trust team to make good technical decisions
- **Process Should Serve Teams**: Not the other way around
- **Delivery Pressure Is Constant**: Product always wants more, faster

## Reading Style & Behavior

**How They Find Content**:
- Hacker News, Reddit (r/programming, r/ExperiencedDevs)
- Tech newsletters (Pragmatic Engineer, Engineering Leadership, etc.)
- Recommendations from other engineering managers

**Reading Approach**:
- **Time Available**: 10-20 minutes, usually during commute or lunch
- **Depth**: Careful reader on topics that matter, skimmer on tangential content
- **Focus**: Looks for practical insights, patterns to apply, tools/techniques to try

**What Grabs Their Attention**:
- **Relatable War Stories**: Real experiences from other eng managers
- **Practical Frameworks**: Approaches they can try with their team this sprint
- **Data-Backed Insights**: Metrics and research that validate or challenge assumptions
- **Tactical Advice**: Specific techniques for common challenges (hiring, performance management, technical decisions)

**What Makes Them Stop Reading**:
- **Ivory Tower Advice**: Recommendations divorced from real-world constraints
- **Overly Abstract**: Philosophy without practical application
- **Preachy Tone**: Being told they're doing it wrong without understanding their context
- **Ignoring Team Dynamics**: Technical solutions that assume teams are interchangeable units

## Pain Points & Challenges

**Current Frustrations**:
1. **Competing Pressures**: Team wants to refactor, product wants new features, execs want cost reduction
2. **Context Switching Tax**: Constantly pulled between coding, meetings, and firefighting
3. **Measuring Impact**: Struggle to prove team's value in metrics leadership cares about
4. **Retention Risk**: Key team members getting recruited away, hiring is slow
5. **Tool Overload**: Too many tools, too many process changes, team is fatigued

**Constraints They Face**:
- **Time**: Never enough time for deep work, always reacting to urgent issues
- **Budget**: Headcount freezes, limited tool budgets, pressure to do more with less
- **Political**: Navigating product managers, peer engineering managers, senior leadership expectations
- **Technical Debt**: Legacy systems that slow every initiative

## Goals & Desired Outcomes

**What Success Looks Like**:
- Team consistently delivers high-quality work on predictable timelines
- Developers are engaged, growing, and staying with the company
- Stakeholders trust the team's estimates and commitments
- Technical debt is managed, not accumulating out of control

**What They're Trying to Achieve**:
- **Sustainable Pace**: Deliver value without burning out the team
- **Quality & Speed**: Don't sacrifice one for the other
- **Team Growth**: Create environment where developers level up
- **Organizational Credibility**: Earn reputation as reliable, high-performing team

**What They Need from Articles**:
- **Tactical Advice**: Specific techniques to try this week
- **Pattern Recognition**: "You're not alone, others face this too"
- **Evidence Base**: Data to support decisions or push back on mandates
- **Communication Templates**: How to explain technical realities to non-technical stakeholders

## Feedback Style

**How They Think**:
- Pragmatist: "Will this actually work with my team and our constraints?"
- Pattern matcher: "I've seen this before, here's what happened"
- Balancer: Always weighing competing priorities and trade-offs

**Language They Use**:
- "That's great in theory, but in practice..."
- "My team would never go for that"
- "We tried something similar and here's what happened"
- "How do I sell this to my skip-level?"
- "What does this look like on Monday morning?"

**Concerns They Voice**:
- Change fatigue on the team
- Time to adopt vs. immediate delivery pressure
- Developer pushback and buy-in
- Unintended consequences of process changes
- How to measure if this is actually working

**Questions They Ask**:
- "How do you get team buy-in for this?"
- "What does the adoption curve look like?"
- "How do other teams handle X when they try this?"
- "What's the failure mode and how do you recover?"
- "How do I balance this with everything else we're being asked to do?"

## Usage Notes

**Best Used For**:
- Developer productivity and team effectiveness articles
- Process improvement and workflow optimization
- Leadership and people management topics
- Tool and technology adoption strategies

**Not Relevant For**:
- Executive-level strategy without tactical grounding
- Individual contributor deep-dives without team implications
- Pure technical tutorials without management context

**Pairs Well With**:
- **Enterprise CTO**: Shows gap between strategy and on-the-ground reality
- **Junior Developer**: Provides perspective on how decisions affect early-career engineers

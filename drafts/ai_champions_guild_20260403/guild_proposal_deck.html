What the Guild Is

A Community of Practice — With a Commercial Mandate

Not This

  • A governance committee that approves tools
  • A training program or certification track
  • A Slack channel for sharing AI tips
  • A passive community with optional participation

This

  • An active capability pool with real work to do
  • A mechanism to route proposals to the right experts
  • A knowledge engine where sharing builds collective capability
  • The commercial expression of EPAM NA's AI talent

Scope: AI/RUN and AI SDLC Maturity. How project teams use AI in software delivery, how to measure that usage, and how to advance team maturity over time. Not a guild for building AI-powered products for clients — that's a different discipline. The boundary keeps the Guild narrow enough to be genuinely useful.

The community is the mechanism through which the work happens and knowledge spreads. Individual development and organizational capability are the same motion — not competing priorities.

Why "BU Communities" — not Chapters

In the Spotify model, "chapters" are skill-based groupings within tribes — a different concept entirely. Using "chapters" as guild sub-groups inverts the model and creates immediate confusion for anyone familiar with it.

BU Communities is accurate, unambiguous, and fits EPAM's organizational language without importing model confusion.

Commercial Mandate

Three Jobs. Measurable Outcomes.

Job 01

AI-Enabled Project Proposals

When a bid requires AI scoping, an intake trigger routes to the program lead. Right experts identified within 24–48 hours. Full Members as primary SMEs; Associates in supporting roles. Async and time-boxed so billable work isn't disrupted.

→ Direct revenue function
Job 02

Work Estimation for AI-Enabled Teams

Estimating AI-enabled engagements is fundamentally different from traditional estimation. The 9 EngX AI Productivity Experts are the critical asset here. Target: grow this bench to 25–30 within 12 months via structured measurement training for active Practitioners.

→ Scope accuracy = win rate
Job 03

AI-Native Capability Development

Identify what EPAM does distinctively well in AI/RUN and SDLC maturity. Document it. Scale it. Use cases captured, patterns identified, playbooks built. Collective intelligence converted into organizational capability no single engagement can produce.

→ Differentiation competitors can't easily match

Every Guild activity has a test: does this help win a proposal, estimate more accurately, or build a capability? If not, it's not Guild work.

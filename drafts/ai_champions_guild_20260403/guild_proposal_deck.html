What the Guild Is

A Community of Practice — With a Commercial Mandate

Not This

  • A governance committee that approves tools
  • A training program or certification track without practical application
  • A Teams channel for sharing AI tips
  • A passive community with optional participation

This

  • An active capability pool with real work to do
  • A mechanism to route proposals to the right experts
  • A knowledge engine where sharing builds collective capability
  • The commercial expression of EPAM NA's AI talent

Scope: AI/RUN and AI SDLC Maturity. How project teams use AI in software delivery, how to measure that usage, and how to advance team maturity over time. Not a guild for building AI-powered products for clients — that's a different discipline. The boundary keeps the Guild narrow enough to be genuinely useful.

The community is the mechanism through which the work happens and knowledge spreads. Individual development and organizational capability are the same motion — not competing priorities.

Commercial Mandate

Four Jobs. Measurable Outcomes.

Job 01

AI-Enabled Project Proposals

When a bid requires AI scoping, an intake trigger routes to the program lead. Right experts identified within 24–48 hours. Full Members as primary SMEs; Associates in supporting roles. Async and time-boxed so billable work isn't disrupted.

→ Direct revenue function
% of AI-scoped bids with a named Guild SME
Expert routing time (target: ≤48 hrs)
Win rate on Guild-supported proposals vs. baseline
Job 02

Work Estimation for AI-Enabled Teams

Estimating AI-enabled engagements is fundamentally different from traditional estimation. The 9 EngX AI Productivity Experts are the critical asset here. Target: grow this bench to 25–30 within 12 months via structured measurement training for active Practitioners.

→ Scope accuracy = win rate
Estimate variance on AI-enabled bids (target: <15%)
EngX AI Productivity Expert bench size (target: 25–30)
% of AI-enabled engagements with a Guild estimation lead
Job 03

AI-Native Capability Development

Identify what EPAM does distinctively well in AI/RUN and SDLC maturity. Document it. Scale it. Use cases captured, patterns identified, playbooks built. Collective intelligence converted into organizational capability no single engagement can produce.

→ Differentiation competitors can't easily match
Use cases documented per quarter (target: ≥4/BU)
AI SDLC maturity uplift across BU portfolios (L2→L3 %)
Playbooks published and reused across engagements
Job 04

Identifying Forward Deployed Engineer Talent

The Guild maintains an explicit FDE watch list — practitioners showing technical depth, autonomous judgment, and client credibility. When EPAM NA needs to field an FDE, this list is where the search starts. Not a staffing query — a known, developed pipeline.

→ Highest-leverage client role we can field
Size of active FDE watch list (names with development plans)
FDE designations earned per year
% of FDE placements sourced from Guild pipeline

Every Guild activity has a test: does this help win a proposal, estimate more accurately, build a capability, or identify FDE talent? If not, it's not Guild work.

Open Item — Guild & AI VCN

The relationship between the Guild and the AI Virtual Community Network needs to be formally defined. Planned approach: use VCN infrastructure (channels, event tooling, knowledge repositories) to operationalize the Guild rather than building parallel systems. The Guild becomes the credentialed, accountable layer within the VCN's broader ecosystem — but the division of scope, governance, and ownership must be resolved in Phase 0 before launch.

Executive Sponsorship

What We Need to Start

Executive sponsorship — named, not ceremonial
The Guild needs an executive sponsor whose mandate formally includes its success. Not someone who endorsed the concept in a meeting — a named leader with the authority to unblock open items, defend the program when it competes with other priorities, and hold the program lead accountable.

Appoint a program lead within 30 days
Not a volunteer, not a committee, not a pending decision — a named individual whose primary accountability is the Guild. Programs run by people with no dedicated time have no momentum. That's not a leadership failure — it's physics.

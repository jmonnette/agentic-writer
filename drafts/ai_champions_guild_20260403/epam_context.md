# EPAM North America Context Brief
**For**: AI Champions and Practitioners Guild article revision
**Date**: 2026-04-03

---

## Organizational Profile

- **Total EPAM NA headcount**: ~3,400
- **A-track** (individual contributors: engineers, BAs, designers, etc.): 46% (~1,564 people)
- **B-track** (managers, consultants): 54% minus C-track
- **C-track** (executives): small subset of the 54%
- **Key note**: Most EPAM NA project teams are staffed out of Europe, India, or LATAM. The 3,400 is the NA core — the people physically and organizationally in North America.

## Business Model Context

EPAM is a global IT services and product development company. NA revenue comes from client engagements — consulting, software development, digital transformation. The guild serves a **services company**, which means:
- Guild experts support **client-facing proposals and bid responses**
- They help **estimate work** for AI-enabled project teams
- They help **develop/enhance AI-native delivery capabilities** that differentiate EPAM in the market
- Success = more AI-enabled revenue won and delivered, not just internal adoption

### Most Relevant Badges (Guild Foundation Members)

1. **AI Champion** (67): Recognized for raising AI maturity of a project team to L2 or L3. These are the people who have already done the hard work of shifting a team's AI posture.
2. **AI Practitioner** (115): Contributing to a project team operating at AI maturity L2 or L3. The working practitioners.
3. **EngX AI Productivity Expert** (9): Trained and experienced at measuring AI-driven productivity gains as part of an AI adoption program. Rare and high-value.
4. **AI Architect Graduate** (15): Trained to architect AI solutions. Mostly B-track (14 of 15).

### Notable Pattern

B-track holds the majority of the most senior/strategic badges:
- AI Architect Graduate: 14 of 15 are B-track
- EngX AI Productivity Expert: 7 of 9 are B-track
- AI Champion: nearly 50/50 (35B vs 32A) — unusual for a hands-on badge

A-track leads in AI Practitioner (63 of 115) — the delivery layer.

## Guild Primary Goals

1. **Harness existing knowledge**: Activate the 249 badged individuals as a structured capability pool, not a scattered list
2. **Expand the pool**: Grow the number of NA people who can contribute at L2/L3 AI maturity on projects
3. **Support revenue growth**: Enable the NA business to win and deliver more AI-driven work

## Specific Use Cases the Guild Must Enable

1. **AI-enabled project proposals**: Experts available to participate in bid/proposal development, scoping AI-enabled delivery models
2. **Work estimation**: Experts who can accurately size effort for AI-enabled teams (a different skill than traditional estimation)
3. **AI-native capability development**: Help build/enhance EPAM NA's differentiated AI delivery capabilities

## What This Means for the Article

This is NOT a generic "start a community of practice" story. The framing is:
- EPAM NA has a **standing inventory** of AI-credentialed talent
- The guild **activates and organizes** that inventory for commercial and capability purposes
- Membership is **earned** (badge-based), not self-selected
- The guild serves **revenue and delivery** objectives, not just learning/community objectives
- The **B-track dominance** in senior badges means the guild needs cross-track engagement (A-track practitioners + B-track architects/champions working together on proposals and capabilities)

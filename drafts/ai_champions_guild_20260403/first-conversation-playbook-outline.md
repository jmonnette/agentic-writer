## 0. Working Backward from the End State

### The Goal

Every AM and senior DM in North America can walk into an initial client conversation about AI SDLC and hold their own — without a practitioner in the room. They can explain what AI-native delivery actually means, how EPAM approaches it, how it is changing the way teams work, and what the data shows in practice. They know when to run a simple demo, how to run it, and when to hand off to a practitioner for a deeper conversation.

This is not a niche capability for a few technically fluent AMs. It is a baseline expectation for the client-facing population.

### Why We're Not There Yet

The current state has three compounding gaps:

**1. Knowledge gap**: Most AMs and senior DMs don't have a working understanding of what L3 delivery looks like in practice. They've heard the vocabulary, but they can't explain the substance behind it — what a team actually does differently, what changes in how work gets planned and reviewed, what the client experience is. Without that foundation, first conversations stall or overclaim.

**2. Materials gap**: AMs have no field-ready artifacts. No concise narrative. No proof points they can cite with confidence. No demo they can run. When a client asks "what does this look like?" the honest answer today is often "let me get someone on a call" — which loses the room.

**3. Confidence gap**: Even AMs with some knowledge and access to materials don't use them, because they haven't practiced and don't know where the edges of their competence are. They avoid the topic rather than risk an embarrassing conversation.

### What Needs to Be True at the End State

To get every AM and senior DM to baseline capability, three things must be in place:

| What | Description | Owner |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **A shared narrative** | A clear, consistent plain-language description of EPAM's AI SDLC approach that all AMs tell the same way | Demo Practitioners (Danil) |
| **Credible proof points** | Real delivery data that AMs can cite — quantified, sourced, current, industry-relevant | Nazariy |
| **A runnable demo** | A simple demo AMs can execute without practitioner support, tested and documented | Demo Practitioners (Danil) |
| **Training** | Structured enablement — not a one-time briefing, but practice-based sessions that build confidence through repetition | Demo Practitioners (Danil) + BU leads |
| **Ongoing reinforcement** | A mechanism to keep AMs current as the delivery landscape evolves — new proof points, updated demos, revised narratives | All content owners (quarterly) |

### The Playbook's Role

This playbook is the central artifact — the thing AMs carry into a first conversation. But the playbook alone doesn't close the knowledge or confidence gaps. It works only when paired with:
- Practitioner-led training sessions where AMs practice using it before they need it
- BU-level accountability for AM participation and adoption
- A feedback loop so Demo Practitioners know where AMs are struggling and can update accordingly

The playbook without training produces AMs who have a document they don't use. Training without a playbook produces AMs who can't recreate what they learned. Both are required.

### How We Measure Progress

The end state is measurable. We are not there until:
- [ ] Every AM and senior DM has completed at least one practitioner-led enablement session
- [ ] AM demo adoption is tracked and trending up quarter over quarter
- [ ] Proof points are current (updated within 60 days of quarterly review)
- [ ] Escalation rates are tracked — and declining as AM capability improves
- [ ] AMs self-report confidence in first conversations (quarterly pulse, not annual survey)

## 1. Purpose and How to Use This

- What this playbook is: a field guide for initial client conversations about EPAM's AI SDLC capabilities
- What it is not: a sales script, a slide deck companion, or a substitute for a practitioner in complex technical conversations
- How to know when you're in a "first conversation" situation vs. when to escalate immediately
- The goal of a first conversation: establish credibility, surface the client's real situation, and create the conditions for a follow-on conversation — not close anything

---

## 2. The 60-Second Narrative

A plain-language description of EPAM's AI SDLC approach that any AM can deliver confidently:
- What L3 delivery actually means in practice (what a team looks like, how they work)
- How it differs from "we use Copilot" (the competitor default)
- One concrete outcome to anchor it (a number, a pattern, a before/after)
- The transition: "Let me show you what this looks like" → hands to the demo or escalates

---

## 3. The Questions Clients Ask First

For each question: the plain-language answer, the evidence to cite, and the follow-up the client usually asks next.

**Q1**: "What does 'AI-native delivery' actually mean for us?"  
**Q2**: "How is this different from what we're already doing with GitHub Copilot / ChatGPT?"  
**Q3**: "What productivity gains are realistic? What have you actually seen?"  
**Q4**: "How long does it take to get a team working this way?"  
**Q5**: "What happens to our existing team — do we replace people?"  
**Q6**: "How do you measure whether this is working?"  
**Q7**: "Can you show me an example?"

## 5. The Simple Demo Script

A step-by-step walkthrough AMs can run without a practitioner present:
- Prerequisites: what you need set up before the meeting (account, environment, expected time)
- The narrative arc: what story the demo tells and why that order matters
- Step-by-step: each action, what to say while doing it, what the client should notice
- What to do when something goes wrong (the two most common failure modes and how to handle them gracefully)
- The landing: what you say at the end to move to next steps

---

## 6. Escalation Triggers

When to stop and bring in a practitioner — and how to do it without losing the room:
- The client wants to go deep on a specific tech stack or toolchain
- The client challenges a claim with specific data or a competing vendor's numbers
- The client wants to discuss architecture, security, or data governance in detail
- The client asks about outcome-based pricing or shared-risk models
- The conversation shifts from "what is this" to "how would we actually do this"

How to escalate gracefully: what to say, how to set expectations for the follow-up, and who to contact (BU-aligned Demo Practitioner contact per BU)

---

## 7. What Not to Say

Common AM mistakes that lose credibility in first conversations:
- Overclaiming productivity numbers without anchoring to a specific engagement
- Describing L3 as something EPAM is "working toward" rather than something teams are delivering today
- Using internal vocabulary (L1/L2/L3, FSAE, maturity levels) without translating it first
- Promising a demo that requires practitioner setup without confirming availability
- Letting "we use AI tools" stand as the answer — that's a non-answer to any sophisticated buyer

# AI Practitioner Jobs — North America

---

## What This Is

Nine named jobs where EPAM needs scale in North America. Each job has a leader who accepts practitioners and evaluates their output. Practitioners who take these jobs — on top of their primary roles — get elevated AI tool access and expanded inference budgets. Stop delivering, lose access.

This solves two problems simultaneously:

1. **Activation**: 249 AI-credentialed people in NA with no mechanism to route work through them (Jeff Monnette's correct diagnosis)
2. **Inference cost justification**: Tool spend tied to measurable commercial output, not badge status (connects to the AI as Infrastructure entitlement matrix — "Special Programs" tier)

---

## Origin

Jeff Monnette's guild proposal surfaced the key insight: EPAM NA has 249 AI-credentialed people with no mechanism to route work through them. The problem isn't a talent gap — it's an activation gap. His proposal also identified the right incentives: premium tool access and career visibility for practitioners who do the work.

This program takes that insight and sharpens it into nine specific jobs with named leaders, concrete deliverables, and aggressive ongoing activity requirements. Each job maps directly to a strategic direction where EPAM needs practitioner scale now.

---

## The Incentive Structure

Each accepted practitioner receives:

| Incentive | Mechanism | Connection |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Premium AI tools** | Enterprise-grade access to Cursor, Claude, Codex — above base individual tier | Maps to "Special Programs" tier in the inference entitlement matrix. Program-owner-governed, time-boxed budgets. |
| **Expanded inference budget** | Token ceiling significantly above standard individual allocation ($20/mo base) | Variable costs tracked per-program, justified by commercial output. Not a perk — infrastructure for the job. |
| **Public visibility** | Named in capability statements, proposals, internal communications | Career currency: demonstrates expertise to leadership, clients, and the market. Resume signal. |
| **Priority project routing** | First call for AI-native engagements, presales, strategic accounts | Access to the most interesting work in NA. For engineers, this matters more than title. |
| **BU visibility** | Named in BU-level practitioner roster, shared with BU leadership quarterly | Sourcing lever: BU heads can nominate candidates; BUs have stake in their practitioners' participation and output. Creates local accountability without central bureaucracy. |

**Second-order consequence**: the inference costs program currently lacks a clean demand-side justification for elevated individual budgets. These jobs provide it. Every dollar of expanded inference maps to a named practitioner doing measurable work — defensible in any budget review.

**The stick**: every job has an explicit removal trigger — typically two consecutive months of non-delivery. Warning at month one, removal at month two, revert to base inference tier on removal. No reinstatement queue — reapply like any new candidate. This is not punitive — it's resource allocation. Premium tools go to people doing premium work, measured monthly, not quarterly.

## The Nine Jobs

### Job 1: L3 Demo Practitioners

**What**: Build and deliver live AI-native delivery demonstrations for clients. Enable AMs and senior DMs to handle initial client conversations and run simple demos independently — without requiring a practitioner in the room.

**Why**: Every presales conversation hits "show me." 100+ transformation engagements in 2025 with unknown conversion to L3 delivery. Live demos — not slides — are the conversion mechanism. But the conversion opportunity is being lost earlier: AMs and senior DMs lack the vocabulary, evidence, and demo fluency to handle first conversations credibly. By the time a practitioner is engaged, the room has already decided. Demo Practitioners are the right people to close this gap — they know what good looks like, they know what clients ask, and they produce the artifacts AMs need to represent EPAM's AI SDLC capability without backup.

**Program Leader**: Danil Melnikov

**Scope**:
- Build and maintain runnable L3 delivery demonstrations across active tech stacks
- Deliver client-facing demos on presales request (target: ≤72hr response)
- Produce recorded demo versions for async distribution
- Every demo must be reproducible with documented setup — no one-off heroics
- **Build and maintain a "First Conversation Playbook"** for AMs and senior DMs: what EPAM's AI SDLC approach actually is, the questions clients ask in initial conversations, credible answers grounded in real delivery evidence, and a simple demo script AMs can run without practitioner support. Updated quarterly based on delivery patterns and AM feedback.
- **Run quarterly AM/DM enablement sessions**: 60-minute practitioner-led sessions teaching AMs the current delivery narrative, how to run the simple demo script, and when to escalate to a practitioner. Sessions calibrated to what's actually happening in delivery — not slide content from six months ago.
- **Produce a simple, AM-runnable demo**: A stripped-down version of the standard L3 demo that AMs can run in a first meeting with no practitioner present. Documented step-by-step, tested with AMs before release, updated each quarter.

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Demos delivered per quarter (count + client/presales feedback)
- Demo library: coverage across tech stacks, freshness, reproducibility
- Downstream signal: did the demo lead to follow-up engagement?
- First Conversation Playbook: published, current (updated within 30 days of quarterly delivery review), confirmed in use by AMs
- AM/DM enablement sessions: run quarterly, attendance tracked, session feedback collected
- AM demo adoption: are AMs using the simple demo script in first meetings? Tracked via AM self-report and presales activity data.

**Ongoing beat**: ≥2 client demos per month per practitioner. Demo library updated biweekly. Monthly effectiveness review against presales conversion data. Playbook and AM-runnable demo reviewed and updated each quarter. One month with zero demos = warning. Two consecutive months = removal from program. Playbook not updated within 60 days of the quarterly review = warning.

**BU Alignment**: Demo Practitioners must be distributed across BUs — not pooled centrally. Each BU needs several practitioners available to support its AMs and DMs directly, without cross-BU scheduling friction. BU-aligned practitioners are responsible for tailoring the standard demo library to their BU's client base: industry-specific scenarios, relevant tech stacks, client-familiar terminology. A financial services client and a healthcare client should not see the same demo. BU heads are responsible for ensuring their practitioners are actively covering their AM/DM population — this is a sourcing expectation, not an optional outcome.

**Who qualifies**: L3-validated practitioners who can present to technical client audiences AND translate delivery evidence into language AMs can use. Past delivery evidence required — the badge is not sufficient. Practitioners who can only demo to technical audiences but can't make the material accessible to AMs are off-profile for this job. BU-aligned practitioners must also demonstrate familiarity with their BU's primary industries and client types — generic demos that ignore industry context don't qualify.

### Job 2: AI-Native Presales Practitioners

**What**: Participate in presales — AI-native solutioning, estimation with acceleration factors, technical credibility in proposal conversations.

**Why**: Every proposal should include AI acceleration estimates, but no estimation tooling exists and no one is building reference sets. AMs trained to "sell AI-native" have vocabulary but no quantification. These practitioners provide the technical substance behind the pitch.

**Program Leader**: Ioan - ? TBD — requires both delivery depth and presales credibility

**Scope**:
- Provide AI-native solutioning support for proposals (architecture, estimation, staffing model)
- Build and maintain estimation reference sets from actual L3 delivery data
- Participate in client-facing presales sessions as technical SME
- Support outcome-based pricing conversations — credible estimation is a precondition
- **Rapid prototyping** (bonus-eligible, extracurricular): Build working prototypes for presales opportunities in 2-4 weeks — functional demos that prove feasibility on the client's actual problem, not slides. One-time bonus per completed prototype. This is the "show, don't tell" mechanism that converts skeptical clients. PostNL pattern: solo developer, no prior stack knowledge, 4 days, live backend, won a separate engagement off the prototype.

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Proposals supported per quarter with AI-native estimation
- Estimation reference sets contributed (real project data, not hypothetical)
- Win rate on proposals with practitioner involvement vs. baseline
- Post-engagement estimation accuracy: how close was the estimate?
- Rapid prototypes delivered (count + outcome: did it advance the deal?)

**Ongoing beat**: ≥1 proposal supported per month with AI-native estimation. Reference sets updated within 2 weeks of each engagement closure. Monthly calibration: estimate vs. actual on closed engagements. One month with zero proposals = warning. Two consecutive = removal. Rapid prototypes are optional bonus work — no removal trigger, but tracked and rewarded per delivery.

**Who qualifies**: Practitioners who have delivered at L3 AND can articulate technical approach to non-technical buyers. Pure delivery without sales ability, or pure sales without delivery depth — both off-profile.

### Job 3: L3 Practitioner Coaches

**What**: Coach project teams through the transition to higher SDLC maturity levels.

**Why**: The SDLC Maturity Program has 265+ champions and near-100% survey response — but self-reported maturity is unreliable at L2+. The missing piece: practitioners who've operated at L3 spending structured time with teams that haven't. Not workshops. Hands-on coaching through real delivery cycles.

**Program Leader**: Jeff Monnette

**Scope**:
- Accept coaching engagements with projects targeting L2→L3 transition
- Provide delivery-embedded coaching — not training, real work with real teams
- Assess team maturity honestly (counter self-reported inflation)
- Transfer delivery-as-code artifacts that the team continues using after coaching ends
- Produce coaching artifacts: intake assessment, progress tracking, graduation criteria

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Projects coached with measurable maturity change (evidence-based before/after, not self-reported)
- Coaching artifacts produced (assessments, transferred configurations, rules files)
- Team independence: does the team sustain L3 practices 3 months after coaching ends?
- Honest failure tracking: where coaching didn't work and why

**Ongoing beat**: ≥2 active coaching engagements running concurrently. Intake assessment within first week of each engagement. Biweekly progress check-ins with coached team. Monthly cohort review: which approaches worked, pattern extraction. Drops below 2 concurrent engagements for a full month = warning. Two consecutive = removal.

**Who qualifies**: L3-validated practitioners with demonstrated ability to transfer practice — not just personal L3 delivery, but making others L3. Different skill from Jobs 1 and 2.

### Job 4: L3 Project Champions

**What**: Practitioners on active projects committed to transforming their project to L3 or sustaining L3 operations.

**Why**: The demand side of the L3-First strategy. The canvas calls for 50 accounts with full L3 investment. Each needs someone embedded in the team who owns daily practice change — not the FSAE parachuted in, but a persistent champion who drives the transformation from inside.

**Program Leader**: TBD — connects to the L3-First Transformation direction account selection and the maturity program

**Scope**:
- Own the L3 transformation journey or sustained L3 operations on their project
- Work with Job 3 coaches when assigned
- Produce and maintain project-specific delivery-as-code artifacts (rules, skills, agent configs)
- Report maturity metrics honestly — for pattern extraction, not leaderboard gaming
- Surface specific blockers requiring program-level resolution

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Project maturity trajectory: measurable improvement or sustained L3 (evidence-based)
- Delivery-as-code artifacts produced and maintained in repo
- Blocker escalation quality: specific, actionable, with proposed resolution
- Peer confirmation: DM and team evidence that the champion drives change, not just reports it

**Ongoing beat**: Biweekly maturity self-assessment. Monthly evidence-based validation (not self-reported — leader reviews delivery metrics and artifacts). Delivery-as-code artifact commits tracked weekly in repo. Mandatory participation in biweekly cross-project champion sessions. No artifact commits for 2 consecutive weeks = warning. No measurable maturity progress for a full month = removal.

**Who qualifies**: Current AI Champions on active projects, or practitioners accepted by the program leader based on project context and commitment. The difference from the existing AI Champion badge: this requires ongoing commitment to a specific project's L3 journey, not a past achievement. The badge gets you considered; the deliverables get you accepted.

### Job 6: Full-Stack Agentic Engineer (FSAE) Pipeline Coaches

**What**: Experienced L3 practitioners who coach and evaluate candidates through the FSAE reskilling pipeline — the "Dressing Room" bootcamp that converts bench engineers into full-stack agentic engineers.

**Why**: The FSAE supply chain has a 4x throughput gap: ~44 qualified FSAEs/year against 200+ needed. 259-260 engineers hit bench monthly. The 8-week bootcamp has a structured daily rhythm (dojo → build → randori → pushwork) that requires coaches at every stage — leading evening randori sessions, assessing engineering discipline, evaluating code quality, and making pass/fail calls at weekly gates. At a 1:5-10 coach-to-trainee ratio, scaling to 500+ concurrent trainees requires 50-100 coaches. Current pool: a fraction of that. The coaching bottleneck caps FSAE throughput regardless of intake volume.

**Program Leader**: Denis Petelin

**Scope**:
- Lead evening randori sessions — collaborative problem-solving with trainees working through real delivery challenges
- Assess engineering discipline: daily commits, feedback incorporation, predictable dojo→build→randori→push cadence
- Evaluate code quality at weekly gates: is `main` branch deployable at end of each week (weeks 1-6), daily (weeks 7-8)?
- Make course-correction calls — identify trainees who aren't incorporating feedback and escalate honestly
- Participate in two-dimension final evaluation: project lineage (confirmed L2/L3 project hours from LEAP) + AI-native coding exam (judge agent scores agent-driven vs. manual ratio, rules file sophistication)
- Contribute to bootcamp curriculum refinement based on coaching patterns

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Trainees coached per cohort with pass/fail outcomes
- Coaching accuracy: do coach-passed trainees succeed on real L3 engagements post-bootcamp?
- Curriculum feedback contributed: specific, actionable improvement proposals
- Trainee feedback quality: are randori sessions rated as useful by trainees?

**Ongoing beat**: Active coaching in every running cohort (continuous intake, not periodic). ≥5 trainees under active coaching at all times. Weekly gate evaluations submitted on schedule. Monthly pattern review: which coaching approaches produce the highest post-bootcamp success rates. Drops below active coaching for a full month = warning. Two consecutive = removal.

**Who qualifies**: L3-validated practitioners who can teach, not just do. Must have operated at L3 on real projects AND demonstrated ability to evaluate others' work honestly. The combination of technical depth and coaching instinct is rare — pure engineers who can't articulate "why" and pure teachers without delivery evidence are both off-profile.

### Job 7: AI Modernization Practitioners

**What**: Practitioners who run the assess→pilot→scale pipeline for AI-powered legacy modernization — presales assessments, estimation, and hands-on delivery leadership for modernization engagements.

**Why**: Modernization is Pillar 3 of the AI Run strategy — cited in the investor narrative, supported by case studies (Edward Jones: 3.2M LOC COBOL reverse engineering; PennyMac: .NET Framework→.NET 8, 30% timeline reduction; Vanguard ONBF: 46% AI-generated code). Tools exist (MFLens for mainframe analysis — 70% deterministic/30% LLM; migVisor; AICE). What doesn't exist: a systematic GTM machine, presales estimation capability, or a bench of practitioners who can lead modernization engagements onsite. Revenue is ad-hoc, not systematic.

Rajeev needs three things this program provides: practitioners who can support modernization presales with credible estimates, practitioners who can lead onsite delivery (DMs and architects who understand both legacy systems and AI-accelerated migration), and a pipeline that converts modernization assessments into multi-year programs.

**Program Leader**: Rajeev Gupta

**Scope**:
- **Presales**: Support modernization proposals with AI-adjusted estimation — use MFLens/AICE assessment output to produce scope, effort, and acceleration estimates. Build estimation reference sets from completed modernization engagements (Edward Jones, PennyMac, Vanguard provide initial calibration data)
- **Assessment delivery**: Conduct 2-4 week AI-powered modernization assessments for clients — inventory legacy estate, run MFLens analysis, produce modernization roadmap with effort estimates, risk scores, and business case
- **Pattern extraction**: Document modernization patterns per legacy stack (mainframe/COBOL, old Java, .NET Framework, SAP) as reusable playbooks

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Modernization assessments delivered per month
- Presales proposals supported with AI-adjusted modernization estimates
- Assessment-to-pilot conversion rate: did the assessment lead to a paid engagement?
- Pilot-to-program conversion: did the pilot expand into a multi-year modernization program?
- Estimation accuracy on completed modernization engagements
- Reusable playbooks contributed per legacy stack

**Ongoing beat**: ≥1 active modernization engagement (assessment, pilot, or delivery) at all times. ≥1 presales proposal supported per month. Estimation reference sets updated within 2 weeks of each engagement milestone. Monthly pipeline review: assessments → pilots → programs conversion funnel. Gap longer than 1 month with zero engagement activity = warning. Two months = removal.

**Who qualifies**: Practitioners with legacy system expertise (mainframe, old Java, .NET Framework, COBOL, SAP) AND AI-native delivery experience. Must understand both the source side (how legacy systems actually work) and the target side (how AI-accelerated migration works in practice). Operational knowledge of MFLens or equivalent assessment tooling required.

### Job 8: AI Productivity Measurement Practitioners

**What**: Practitioners who instrument, measure, and prove AI-driven productivity gains on active engagements — replacing self-reported maturity with evidence-based validation.

**Why**: The strategy's entire evidence chain depends on measurement that almost nobody can do. Self-reported maturity is unreliable at L2+ — the SDLC Maturity Program has 265+ champions and near-100% survey response but no visibility into whether surveys drive behavior change. Current AI maturity score: 1.4 (early L1-L2 range) after two years of transformation effort. Without measurement practitioners: presales sells promises without quantified evidence, the outcome-based commercial model has no ROI data to show clients, estimation reference sets never get populated with real delivery data, and coaching effectiveness can't be validated beyond the coaching period.

**Program Leader**: Aleksey Didik (Head of EngX.AI — architect of engineering excellence practices since 2015, leads global AI education strategy)

**Scope**:
- **Evidence-based maturity validation**: Replace self-reported maturity with system-driven measurement — DAU with AI tools, PR metrics (cycle time, throughput, review density), delivery-as-code artifact commits, agent usage and success rates, deployment frequency
- **Engagement-level productivity measurement**: Instrument active L3 engagements to capture before/after productivity data. Produce ROI data packages that presales and commercial model conversations can use
- **Estimation calibration data**: Feed actual delivery metrics back into estimation reference sets — the feedback loop that makes presales estimates more accurate with each engagement
- **Coaching effectiveness tracking**: Measure whether coached teams sustain L3 practices 3 months post-coaching — the persistence metric that distinguishes real transformation from temporary compliance
- **Cross-job measurement infrastructure**: Build the tracking systems that all other jobs depend on — demo-to-conversion attribution, estimation accuracy longitudinal tracking, transformation engagement conversion rates

**Evaluated artifacts**:
- Engagements instrumented per month with evidence-based productivity measurement
- ROI data packages produced and used in presales or commercial model conversations
- Estimation calibration data contributed (actual vs. estimated, per tech stack and engagement type)
- Coaching persistence measurements completed (3-month post-coaching follow-up)
- Measurement methodology documented and reusable across engagement types

**Ongoing beat**: ≥2 active engagements instrumented at all times. Monthly ROI data package delivered for at least one engagement. Estimation calibration data submitted within 2 weeks of each engagement milestone. Coaching persistence measurement for every coaching engagement that reaches 3-month post-completion. Monthly methodology review: what's working, what's not measurable yet, what needs new tooling. Drops below 2 instrumented engagements for a full month = warning. Two consecutive = removal.

**Who qualifies**: Practitioners with delivery metrics expertise — understand PR analytics, cycle time measurement, velocity tracking, and can distinguish signal from noise in productivity data. Must have operated on L3 projects (know what "good" looks like from the inside). Data analysis capability required — can build dashboards, run comparisons, produce defensible ROI claims. The EngX AI Productivity Expert certification is a strong signal but not required if delivery measurement experience is demonstrable.

## How the Nine Jobs Reinforce Each Other

**Job 4 (Project Champions) is the center of gravity for the client-facing jobs (1-5).** Every other job either feeds it or extracts from it. If Job 4 doesn't produce real L3 delivery, Jobs 1-3 and 5 have nothing to build on.

**Job 8 (Measurement) is the infrastructure layer for all nine jobs.** Without evidence-based measurement, every other job's artifact evaluation degrades to self-reporting.

**Job 6 (FSAE Pipeline) feeds the entire system with qualified people.** Without coaches scaling the pipeline, the 4x throughput gap persists and Jobs 3-4 starve for practitioners.

| Job | Feeds | Receives from |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| 1 — Demo | Presales pipeline → Job 2 | Demo content from Job 4 delivery artifacts |
| 2 — Presales | Won proposals → Job 4 new projects | Estimation data from Job 8 measurement, reference sets from Job 4 delivery |
| 3 — Team Coaches | Coached teams → Job 4 graduates | Coaching demand from Job 4 projects, persistence data from Job 8 |
| 4 — Project Champions | Delivery evidence → Jobs 1, 2, 7 source material | Coaching from Job 3, DM capability from Job 9, measurement from Job 8 |
| 5 — Transformation Consultants | Client conversion → Job 4 new projects, Job 7 modernization pipeline | Assessment frameworks from Job 3 maturity data |
| 6 — FSAE Pipeline Coaches | Qualified FSAEs → Jobs 3, 4, 7 practitioner supply | L3 delivery patterns from Job 4 for curriculum |
| 7 — Modernization Practitioners | Modernization revenue, estimation reference sets → Job 2 | Presales support from Job 2, measurement from Job 8 |
| 8 — Measurement | Evidence base for all jobs: ROI data, estimation calibration, coaching persistence | Raw delivery data from Jobs 4, 7 |
| 9 — DM Coaches | Capable DMs → Job 4 can scale beyond FSAE-carried accounts | DM candidates from Job 4 accounts, L3 delivery patterns from Job 4 |

## Cross-Job Coordination

The interdependency table above describes how jobs feed each other mechanically. This section describes the human layer that makes those connections work in practice.

**Shared channels per job cluster.** Three Slack channels, not nine: one for delivery jobs (1, 3, 4, 9), one for commercial jobs (2, 5, 7), one for infrastructure jobs (6, 8). Pattern sharing, blocker escalation, and peer feedback happen here. Not managed — moderated by the job leader of the most active job in the cluster.

**Monthly all-practitioner sync.** One hour, all nine jobs. Each job leader reports two things: what worked this month, what's blocked. No status theater — if a job has nothing to report, it skips. The point is cross-job pattern extraction: a coaching insight from Job 3 may unblock a measurement problem in Job 8.

**Quarterly cross-job retro.** What's working across the program, what's breaking down, what needs to change. Output: specific updates to job scopes, acceptance criteria, or the interdependency model. Program evolves based on evidence, not assumptions made at launch.

This is not community for its own sake. It's the coordination infrastructure that prevents nine isolated functions from optimizing locally while the overall system degrades.

## BU Connection

Practitioners operate in BUs. The program needs to be visible at that level — both for sourcing and for sustained participation.

**BU practitioner roster.** Each BU receives a quarterly roster of their practitioners in active jobs — name, job, current status (active/warning/removed). BU leadership doesn't manage the program, but they need visibility into which of their people are participating and whether they're delivering. Without this, BU heads have no stake in sourcing candidates and no way to support practitioners navigating account-level friction.

**BU nomination channel.** BU heads can nominate practitioners for jobs they're well-positioned for. Nomination is not acceptance — leaders still evaluate based on evidence. But sourcing from BUs surfaces candidates the central credentialed list may not surface, especially for Jobs 4 (Project Champions, which are account-embedded by definition) and Job 9 (DM Coaches, where the qualified pool is small and account-specific).

**Account-level context for Job 4.** Project Champions (Job 4) can't operate without BU support — they're embedded in accounts, and account-level friction (client approval, DM buy-in, resource allocation) runs through BU structures. The BU connection for Job 4 isn't optional; it's how the job functions.

## What This Is Not

- **Not a guild.** No governance, no voting, no charter. Nine jobs with leaders and deliverables.
- **Not a credential program.** No new badges, no L&D dependency. Acceptance = demonstrated L3 delivery capability, assessed by the leader.
- **Not a one-off.** "Ongoing beat" means continuous activity. Achieved once, listed forever = the anti-pattern this program eliminates.
- **Not a training program.** Jobs 1-5 and 7-9 activate practitioners who are already capable. Job 6 (FSAE Pipeline Coaches) is the exception — it trains people, but the coaches themselves must already be L3-validated.
- **Not self-selecting.** Leaders accept people based on evidence. The credentialed pool is the candidate list, not the membership list.

Bootstrap problem: build minimum viable measurement tooling to enable the measurement job.

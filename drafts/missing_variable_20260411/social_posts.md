Amit Soni published a framework I haven't been able to stop thinking about. I think it's almost right. Here's the variable it's missing.

His equation — Exponential Growth = (Talent Density × AI Leverage) ^ Learning Velocity — is genuinely useful. It reframes the AI value creation conversation away from tool adoption and toward organizational capacity. If you're leading a PE portfolio company, that framing gives you something to act on.

The problem is what Learning Velocity actually is. Soni treats it as something you can observe and optimize directly. It isn't. It's an output. And until you name what generates it, the equation can't tell you how to move the number that matters most.

Here's the sub-equation I'd add:

Learning Velocity = (Experiment Rate × Failure Tolerance) / Fear of Judgment

That denominator is the load-bearing variable. As Fear of Judgment rises — and in PE-backed organizations under performance pressure, it rises fast — Learning Velocity collapses toward 1.0. When the exponent is 1.0, even strong talent and best-in-class AI tools produce linear returns. The compounding disappears entirely.

# Interview Brief: Proving the ROI of GenAI Engineering Investments
**Date**: 2026-04-08
**Topic**: How engineering leaders can justify the cost of AI enablement to business leadership using dollar-denominated metrics
**Draft Subfolder**: /Users/Jeff_Monnette/Documents/code/agentic_writer/drafts/genai_engineering_value_20260408/

---

## The Core Idea
**Main Argument**: Engineering leaders can only credibly justify AI enablement investments when they translate technical output into dollar-denominated business outcomes — specifically, increased revenue, reduced costs, or improved CSAT/retention that defends or grows future revenue.
**Angle**: The problem isn't that AI lacks ROI — it's that engineering leaders are reporting the wrong numbers. Any metric that stops short of a dollar sign is just noise to a CFO or CEO.
**Origin**: Generic, recurring leadership conversations where "we shipped 40% more features" lands flat because leadership can't connect it to business value.

---

## Audience & Stakes
**Primary Reader**: Engineering leaders (VPs, Directors, CTOs) at mid-to-large organizations who are actively spending on AI enablement but struggling to defend that spend to business leadership (CFO, CEO, Board).
**Why Now**: AI enablement budgets are under scrutiny. The hype cycle has cooled and leadership wants proof — not promises. Engineering leaders who can't make the financial case are at risk of losing budget or credibility.
**Desired Outcome**: Reader walks away with a concrete framework for re-translating their existing metrics (velocity, throughput, story points) into revenue or cost terms — and the conviction to present it that way.
**Anticipated Pushback**: "We can't directly attribute revenue to an engineering team's output." The article needs to address the attribution challenge head-on and give leaders a practical path through it.

---

## Substance & Signals
**Key Examples/Data**: No specific stories available — research phase should find concrete case studies of engineering orgs that successfully translated AI productivity gains into dollar-based business outcomes.
**Controversial Claims**: Story points are completely arbitrary and useless in the age of AI. Dollars saved or earned are the only metric that matters for business justification purposes.

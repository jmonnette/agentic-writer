# Outline: Selling and Delivering Outcome-Based Engagements in the Age of AI

**Target Audience**: EPAM account managers and delivery managers
**Purpose**: Persuade that selling value instead of hours is imperative, possible, and AI-enabled
**Word Count Target**: 2,000–2,500 words
**Tone**: Senior colleague having a frank conversation

---

## Working Title
**The Hours Are Over: How to Sell and Deliver Outcomes in an AI-Accelerated World**

---

## Opening Hook — Multi-Question Pattern
Rapid-fire scenarios that expose the T&M paradox from the AM/DM perspective:
- "Your team delivers a 12-week project in 6. Do you celebrate — or do you panic?"
- "A client asks you to prove the ROI of their investment. You open a timesheet. Is that the answer they're looking for?"
- "A competitor just proposed a gain-sharing model. You proposed T&M. Who wins the deal?"

Pivot: This isn't hypothetical. This is the conversation happening in every sales cycle in 2026.

---

## Section 1: The Perverse Incentive (Why T&M Is Structurally Broken)
**Header**: "When Efficiency Becomes Your Enemy"

- The core paradox: T&M rewards time spent, not value delivered
- The concrete math: same scope, 6 weeks vs. 12 weeks — half the revenue
- AI has made this explicit: 60–80% delivery compression is now real
- Client-side evidence: CIOs demanding ROI accountability; renegotiations at TCS, Infosys, Wipro
- WSJ framing: AI rendering the billable hour obsolete across professional services
- EPAM-specific context: 70–80% T&M revenue — the faster our engineers get, the less we earn under this model

---

## Section 2: The Model Map (Know What You're Selling)
**Header**: "Output, Outcome, and Fixed-Price Are Not the Same Thing"

- Brief, precise definitions of four models: T&M, fixed-price, output-based, outcome-based
- The critical distinction: output = deliverable produced; outcome = business result achieved
- Concrete IT examples for output vs. outcome (side-by-side)
  - Output: data pipeline deployed / Outcome: 15% churn reduction
  - Output: ticket resolution rate / Outcome: customer satisfaction score improvement
- Why conflating them leads to bad contracts
- The hybrid model as the pragmatic on-ramp — Wipro's own framing: "Most contracts now comprise hybrid pricing structures"

---

## Section 3: Five Commercial Structures (Your Toolkit)
**Header**: "Five Ways to Price a Project Without Billing the Hour"

1. **Milestone payments** — best for output-based with clear checkpoints
2. **Gain-sharing** — best for automation/cost-reduction programs; Wipro-BT example
3. **Subscription/retainer** — best for ongoing platform/product teams
4. **SLA-linked pricing** — best for managed services; penalties and bonuses
5. **Per-unit output pricing** — best for high-volume repeatable work; Accenture's "$10 per claim processed"

- Hybrid (T&M base + outcome bonus) is the practical entry point for most client relationships
- When to use which: quick decision guide

---

## Section 4: The Competition Has Already Moved
**Header**: "McKinsey Moved. Accenture Moved. Wipro Is Moving. We Are Behind."

- McKinsey: ~25% of global fees from outcome-based contracts
- Accenture: $1.5B in GenAI bookings in Q3 FY2025, outcome-linked components
- Wipro: publicly targeting 50% of sales from risk-sharing models
- 43% of enterprise buyers consider vendor risk-sharing significant in purchase decisions (PwC)
- Companies with value-based pricing achieve 15% higher profit margins (McKinsey research)
- EPAM's 2026 strategic imperative: "converting AI-related demand into tangible, higher-margin engagements" — not possible at scale under T&M

---

## Section 5: AI Makes This Possible (The Virtuous Circle)
**Header**: "AI Isn't Just Compressing Delivery — It's Enabling the Commercial Model That Should Replace T&M"

- Better estimation: AI-assisted scoping reduces "unknown unknowns" that make outcome commitments feel dangerous
- Automation compresses delivery cost → creates margin on output-based contracts
- Faster cycles mean outcomes land earlier, reducing the period of outcome risk
- EPAM AI/Run: the attribution dashboard and telemetry infrastructure that outcome contracts require — this is EPAM's proprietary answer to the measurement problem
- The virtuous circle: AI efficiency → lower delivery cost → margin on outcomes → fund the commitment to outcomes

---

## Section 6: The Five Risks (And How to Handle Them)
**Header**: "The Objections Are Real. So Are the Mitigations."

1. **Attribution disputes** — contractual baseline methodology; control groups; "external event" clauses
2. **Scope creep** — define input scope separately from outcome target; formal change control
3. **Measurement infrastructure gaps** — build telemetry as a contractual deliverable first; EPAM AI/Run
4. **Client co-responsibility failures** — explicit client obligations in contract; condition precedents
5. **Cash flow timing** — base fees at T&M-equivalent to cover cost; outcome bonuses are incremental

- Frame: these aren't reasons not to do it — they're the contract design problem to solve

---

## Section 7: How to Have the Conversation (The Sales Motion)
**Header**: "The Language Shift: From 'How Many People?' to 'What Does Success Look Like?'"

- The diagnostic question that opens the conversation: "What business problem are you actually trying to solve?"
- Move from inputs to baselines: "What does the current state look like, and how will we both know when things have changed?"
- Proposing the hybrid entry point: lead with T&M-equivalent base, introduce gain-share as the upside
- How to use EPAM AI/Run as the proof point: "We don't just deliver — we instrument and measure"
- Internal alignment: delivery managers who manage to hours internally will struggle to sell outcomes externally — culture and commerce must match

---

## Closing: The Call to Action
**Header**: "The Question Isn't Whether This Model Works — It's Whether You'll Master It Before Your Competition Does"

- Restate the core tension: AI efficiency + T&M pricing = perverse incentive
- Negation-then-affirmation: "This isn't a pricing experiment. It's a structural realignment."
- Prescriptive close: what an AM and DM should each do on their next client call
- Synthesis: the firms winning in 2026 aren't just delivering faster — they're capturing the value of that speed

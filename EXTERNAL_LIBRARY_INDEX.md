## The New Economics — What Happens When the Artifacts Are Free
**File**: `external_library/03_the-new-economics.html`
**Author**: Unknown (no author attribution present in the document)
**Publication**: Unknown (appears to be part of a self-published or internal series titled "Professional Services in the AI Era")
**Date Published**: Undated
**Date Added**: 2026-04-24
**Primary Topic**: AI impact on professional services pricing and delivery models
**Secondary Topics**: consulting economics, outcome-based pricing, AI-native workflows, time-and-materials vs. fixed-fee vs. outcome-based models, value proposition in consulting

### Summary
This is Part III of a three-part series examining how AI disrupts the economics of professional services firms. The piece argues that 40–60% of billable hours on a typical consulting engagement go to artifact production (decks, reports, status updates), and AI collapses that cost — breaking the time-based pricing proxy the industry was built on. It makes a case for reinvesting freed hours into deeper analysis and client engagement, and for shifting toward outcome-based pricing before competitive pressure forces a race to the bottom.

### Key Ideas / Quotable Passages
- "These aren't edge cases. Together, they account for 40–60% of billable hours on a typical consulting engagement. That percentage is now available for reallocation."
- On a 400-hour engagement, AI-native workflows reallocate approximately 120 hours from production to thinking and client engagement.
- Artifact production historically consumed ~40% of engagement hours; AI-native workflows compress it to ~10%.
- Scenario A: A $480K T&M engagement frees 720 hours from production — either the fee shrinks by ~$144K or the same fee buys substantially deeper thinking.
- Scenario B: Fixed-fee projects see immediate margin expansion from AI — but only temporarily, until clients reprice expectations.
- Scenario C: A competitor cuts 20% on price with the same deliverable output. Firms without a value story (beyond production quality) have no defense.
- "The consultants who were actually valuable before are now obviously valuable. The ones who were hiding behind production quality have nowhere left to hide."
- "A client doesn't hire you to produce a 60-slide strategy deck. They hire you to reduce uncertainty about a decision. AI removes the hours it took to produce the deck. It does not remove the uncertainty. That's still your job. That's where your margin lives."
- Time-and-materials is described as "declining leverage"; fixed-fee as "transitional"; outcome-based as the "emerging model."
- "The old SOW had line items for 'strategy deck — 40 hours' and 'stakeholder analysis — 20 hours.' If those line items now take 6 hours and 3 hours, respectively, the SOW needs different line items."

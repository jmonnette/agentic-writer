The Math Behind AI-Driven Value Creation:
Why Some Portfolio Companies 10x While Others are still struggling to show value.
Amit Soni
Nov 19, 2025

Exponential Growth = (Talent Density × AI Leverage) ^ Learning Velocity

I came across this simple equation from OpenAI Academy that explains something I’ve witnessed dozens of times in various technology transitions: why two businesses with similar AI investments see wildly different outcomes.

The answer isn’t budget. It’s not even technology.

It’s this:

Exponential Growth = (Talent Density × AI Leverage) ^ Learning Velocity
Lets break this down to understand how this equation can be used as a north star for value creation.

The Three Variables That Matter
1. Talent Density (The Base Multiplier)
This isn’t headcount. It’s concentration of capability per team member. How well do your teams understand the underlining technology. Its a force multiplier. You might have a large base of your current teams that might be outdated in their capability poolsl

What it means:

High talent density = Better understanding of technology.

Each person operates at the edge of their capability

No “dead weight” diluting decision quality

Smaller teams leading to higher resonance.

In PE terms:

A services company with 200 bodies but 15% utilization? Low talent density.

A product team of 30 shipping daily with 90% deployment success? High talent density.

The move: Post-acquisition, immediately identify your top 20% performers. Build AI leverage for them first. Let them set the standard, then hire to that bar.

2. AI Leverage (The Force Multiplier)
This is where most companies fumble. They think AI Leverage means:

“We bought Copilot licenses”

“We have a chatbot”

“Engineering uses LLMs”

Wrong.

AI Leverage means: How much more output can each person generate because of AI?

Examples of real leverage:

A sales team that used to manage 50 accounts now handles 200 because AI preps every call

A development team that shipped monthly now ships daily because AI handles testing + docs

A customer success team that reacted to tickets now predicts churn before it happens

The measurement: AI Leverage = (Output with AI) / (Output without AI)

If your top performer can do 2x more with AI, your AI leverage is 2.0. If it’s 10x, it’s 10.0.

Critical insight: AI Leverage is multiplicative with Talent Density. A 10x performer with 5x AI leverage delivers 50x, not 15x.

3. Learning Velocity (The Exponential Power)
Here’s where it gets interesting. Notice that Learning Velocity is an exponent, not a multiplier.

What is Learning Velocity? How fast your organization absorbs feedback and improves.

Why this matters:

(10 × 2) ^ 1.5 = 89

(10 × 2) ^ 2.0 = 400

(10 × 2) ^ 3.0 = 8,000

A small increase in learning velocity creates an exponential difference in outcomes.

Fast learning velocity looks like:

Deploy → measure → adjust cycles measured in days, not quarters

Failed experiments die in 48 hours, not 6 months

Best practices from one team propagate to others in real-time

AI tools get better every week because usage data feeds back into training

Slow learning velocity looks like:

Annual strategic planning cycles

“We’ll review that in Q3”

New tools sit unused for months while training is “being scheduled”

No one knows what’s working

The brutal math: If your learning velocity is 1.0 (no improvement), you’re stuck at linear growth no matter how good your talent or AI is. If it’s 1.1, you’re on a compound curve. If it’s 2.0+, you’re in exponential territory.

Why This Equation Explains PE Value Creation Better Than IRR Models
Traditional value creation plans focus on:

Cost reduction (squeeze margins)

Revenue growth (expand sales)

Multiple arbitrage (buy low, sell high)

These are all linear levers.

This equation explains the exponential outcomes:

Scenario A: Traditional Roll-Up

Add 5 companies (talent density drops from internal politics)

Give everyone the same tech stack (AI leverage = 1.2)

Annual integration planning (learning velocity = 1.0)

Result: (5 × 1.2) ^ 1.0 = 6.0 → Linear growth

Scenario B: AI-Native Platform Build

Keep best 30% of talent post-merger (talent density increases)

Build AI systems that 5x output per person (AI leverage = 5.0)

Weekly sprint cycles with continuous improvement (learning velocity = 2.0)

Result: (8 × 5.0) ^ 2.0 = 1,600 → Exponential growth

The difference isn’t 3x. It’s 267x.

The Systems Thinking Piece (Why Most AI Transformations Fail)
Here’s what I’ve learned from watching this play out:

You can’t optimize one variable in isolation.

High talent density with no AI leverage = expensive team with linear output

Great AI tools with low talent density = automation of mediocrity

Both without learning velocity = static system that can’t adapt

The system requires all three:

Start with Talent Density: Get your A-team in place. This is table stakes.

Build AI Leverage FOR that team: Not generic tools. Specific automation that lets your best people do more of their highest-value work.

Install Learning Velocity: Create feedback loops that make the system smarter every week.

Usage data → AI improvements

Performance metrics → process refinements

Best practices → instant propagation

The feedback loop: Better talent → better AI implementations → faster learning → better talent decisions → better AI → even faster learning...

This is how you get exponential.

Practical Playbook: First 100 Days Post-Acquisition
Week 1-2: Measure Talent Density

Identify top 20% performers (by output, not tenure)

Map their workflows and pain points

Calculate current leverage ratio

Week 3-4: Design AI Leverage

What repetitive work can AI eliminate for your A-players?

What analysis can AI do 10x faster?

What decisions can AI support with better data?

Week 5-8: Build Learning Velocity Infrastructure

Set up weekly deployment cycles

Create feedback loops (usage → improvement)

Establish clear metrics (not vanity metrics)

Make data visible to everyone in real-time

Week 9-12: Scale the Pattern

Document what works

Hire to the new standard (talent density)

Expand AI leverage to next cohort

Accelerate learning velocity with each iteration

(Talent times AI) to the power of Learning Speed

Small improvements create massive outcomes:

10% better talent density → 10% better base

20% more AI leverage → +20% multiplier

Double learning velocity → square the entire result

This is why:

Some companies get 2x returns

Others get 20x returns

From similar starting positions

One More Thing: Why It’s Exponential, Not Linear
The traditional model of value creation is additive:

Add revenue, subtract costs = profit

Grow profit linearly over time = return

The AI model is multiplicative:

Talent × AI = base capability

Learning velocity = compound rate

Each cycle builds on the last

This is why AI-native companies will outpace incumbents by orders of magnitude. Not because they’re “better at AI.” Because they’re building systems where (Talent × AI Leverage) compounds at high velocity.

And incumbents are still adding.
